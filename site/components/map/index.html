What is a map component?

The demand of mobile technology has enabled maps to become an essential interface component in a wide range of applications. It is necessary that the map component is easy to navigate, because everyone uses maps via technology to navigate their every move throughout the city.

Typical Appearance

Across platforms, maps demonstrate a number of common characteristics in their appearance. Maps are presented with custom annotations, highlighted regions, and overlays. Users have full control to pan, rotate, zoom, and can explore maps in 3D and 2D. One can change the map settings from map, transit, and satellite. The maps also give users a sense of direction, where users can view their current location or can guide them to their next destination using directions APIs.

Variants :

Embedded Maps appear very similar to a full-featured map implementation, but typically restrict the availability of user interactions. For example, embedded maps on Thrillist.com do not permit the user to search for locations other than bars and restaurants. Users can only drop pins or can get directions to the location. These behaviors vary from site to site. It is clear that Thrillist is best known to find the hottest bars and restaurants near your location.

Waze is the largest community based traffic and navigation app. The app has implemented a map to show the user the location and the direction to take when driving. The app gathers data from drivers who are navigating through a certain route and assist with finding the most efficient way to reach your destination. Waze's maps are also created in real time, which means that if you are the first person to drive down a single road, you will create that road on Waze's map. The map reports: traffic, accidents, speed limits, road conditions, policemen on the highways, gas stations, and suggests songs from spotify. The interface was also created for a quick glance at a map, which means that the interface is easy to read and is not cluttered. It is clear that the most effiect map to use while driving is waze, because the fastest route is constantly updating to the most effiecient route.

Uber utilizes the integrated maps for the drivers and the users. The users use the integrated maps to set a pick up/drop off location by typing in an adress, by moving a pin to a location. Uber has improved the maps for drivers by updating the route in real time, requiring less app switching and less distractions for the driver. The maps show real time traffic, lane guidance, and night mode. Users who are resquestion a ride use the map to request a ride and can view the map to predict and can view the current driver location.

Priority Metrics

Learnability

The learnability measure is quite an easy task for most maps that are integrated within our technology. Map implementation is up to date and is quite easy to learn. Developers have created maps to be user friendly by implementing several buttons that a first time user can quickly decide to click to perform a certain task. Once a user learns where all of the key components of the map are, the user will automatically know where to go. Maps are apart of everyones lives, therefore learnabaility needs to be simple for map components.

Efficiency

Efficiency must be highly prioritized by any implementation of a map component. Especially in mobile maps, users will require practically instant access to directions. If an experienced user has difficulty accessing geographic information they will likely become frustrated and possibly migrate to a different map application. In addition, if maps were inefficient the technological maps could cause trouble on the highways, by having a user spend to much time trying to understand a map. Therefore, it is critical for a map implementation to be effiecint with finding quick tasks, directions, and fast routes.

Memorability

Most technology users access geographic information on a regular basis. The importance of memorability is again reduced by the relative simplicity of most map interfaces. Maps should not require too much memorization of operations for the typical user. The memorability metric may be considered more important in complex map implementations such as arcGis, but people tend to use such applications in a professional capacity; meaning that their use will likely be more regular and thus memorability is a less important measure of usability.

Errors

A low error rate characterizes a properly usable implementation of a map interface. This can be attributed to the prevalance of map components on mobile technologies. Mobile users of maps may be in motion when making use of a map interface, meaning that the safety of the user and of those around them could be compromised with each additional unexpected result.

Satisfaction

User satisfaction is always an important measure of usability, and maps are no different. Developers must prioritize satisfaction in order to differentiate their implementation from those of their competitors. Everyone has their own preferance on certain map components. The top highest features for maps satisfaction include: the fastest route, uncluttered maps, real time map updates, traffic, and quick and easy to find features.

Key Characteristics

Feedback

Maps are one of the most useful components to navigate your way around. In order to make users understand the effects of their actions, any and all feedback must convey all relevant information in a manner that is visible and simple. For example, users must never doubt that they positively triggered the directions feature by typing in their depart location and their destination location. If users cancel the triggering direction feature, due to not wanting the direction route open; the action then triggers and disables that deature at the moment.

Visibility

Some elements of visibility can be considered feedback. Users must be able to easily identify both the features of the map itself and the signifiers of actions available to the user at any given time. Maps communicate so much information at once. The ability of the user to discern what they can and cannot interact with, but with the time remaining is capable of interpreting the geographic information of the map.

Intuitive Affordances

Maps communicate fundamental and physical information. Thus interactions with a well formed map component should mimic interactions with a physical object. Intuitive affordances is important because maps need to align with the mental model of maps for the users. This type of behavior can be seen in almost all popular map implementations. For example, Google Maps allow users to pan around the map as though they were manipulating a physical object, rather than only being able to commit the task with arrow keys or a button.

Typical Behavior

The primary function of any embedded map is to demonstrate some location in space. Maps implement behaviors such as:

# Behavior Description
1 Movement The ability to zoom, pan, and rotate the map (sometimes in 3-D space).
2 Search the ability to search for particular locations in the map
3 Marking the ability to point to and mark locations of interest
4 Directions maps typically implement a routing function that will display to the user a route between two points
5 Snap to Current Location maps allow the user to snap to their immediate surroundings

Events

An event is something that can happen to a user interface component. They are both conceptual and highly concrete in that events very frequently translate directly into a user interface component’s API.
Panning

The Grab: initiated by mouse button down

The grab event is initiated when the user clicks and holds their click at any non-special location on the map. The Grab is accompanied by a small piece of feedback in the form of a cursor change (at least on macOS).

The Pan: Maintained while mouse button down

While the user has grabbed the map, one may move their mouse in any direction to have the map pan in the direction of the movement. The movement of the map essentially happens in real time along with the movement of the mouse.

The Release: initiated by mouse button release

User controlled map panning is completed when the user releases their mouse button. Depending on the implementation, the map may continue to move in the direction of its momentum when the mouse was released.

View Style Change

When the user clicks the element designated to change viewing style (satellite, map, and transit), the map will rerender in the chosen style if not already in that state.

3D View Toggle
When the user clicks on the 3D/2D view toggle button, the viewing style of the map will toggle to the opposite state. This event may be reversed by clicking the button again.
Snap to Current Location
When the user clicks the snap to current location button, the map will immediately pan and zoom to the user's current location.
Zoom In/Zoom Out
When the user clicks on the zoom in or out button, the map will animate a change in display that corresponds either to a larger or smaller scale (typically there are limits imposed upon this scale). Other applications allow for different ways to initiate the zoom event (scrolling, holding a zoom button, etc.).
Search

For the basic functionality of the search function, click here

Result Events: initiated by submit

Upon successful search submission, an embedded map will return either a list of relevant results (less specific search term), or will snap directly to the location of a specific search term. In either case, the map will display feedback in the form of a marker which indicates the positions of the returned results.

Snap to Result: initiated by click

When the user clicks on a result returned by search, the map will display additional information about the location. In addition, some implementations can trigger the "snap to" event to a particular location.

Drop Pin

Drop Pin: initiated by aclick and a hold click

The drop pin event allows the user to mark a specific point of interest on the map. Which allows for the behaviors to act upon that location (e.g. get directions to that location).
Get Directions

Get Directions to/from Specified Location: initiated by click

The get directions to and from a specified location event presents a menu where the user must enter the opposite end of their journey (origin/destination respectively). This location may be selected either by a Drop Pin event or by a search result.

Origin/Destination Selection: initiated by enterText

The origin and destination selection event takes a text input from the user in the corresponding text input box. As the user inputs the text; the relevant location options will display. The user must enter an exact location or select from the presented location options to finalize their origin and destination selection.

Display Directions: initiated by submit

The event causes the map to draw a route between a chosen starting location and the destination. Along with displaying the recommended route, the map will additionally present the user with an estimated time the route will take. Most map implementations display several different routes. Routes differentiate depending on the user's preference for transportation such as: walking, public transit, or driving (e.g. Google and Apple Maps)

The Get Directions event is triggered when the user clicks the directions button, the appearance varies by platform. Upon click, the map component presents the user with two text input boxes: one for the origin location and one for the destination.

Origin and Destination Selection: initiated by enterText

Each text input partially implements the search function described earlier so as to facilitate input entry and location selection.

Diplay Directions

The event causes the map to draw a route between a chosen starting location and the specified location upon submission of these locations. Along with displaying the recommended route, the map will additionally present the user with an estimated time tfor the route.

Platform-Specific Instances

macOS
Apple's proprietary map implementation makes use of macOS. Specific features allow users to manipulate maps in interesting ways. These features can be seen in the following events:

Zoom In/Out

Initiated and maintained by pull (two fingers outward) and pinch (two fingers inward) respectively. The behavior of the zoom is essentially the same as in any other map, but the input provides the user with greater tactile feedback than other applications.

Rotate

Initiated and maintained by: two finger rotate. Rotation operates the same as in other applications, but can be triggered in Apple Maps not only by clicking the corresponding button on the interface itself, but also by a more tactile operation.

Drop Pin

Initiated by force click. The drop pin can be initiated in Apple maps via force click, a proprietary operation.

iOS11

iOS 11 for iPhone has improved several features for Maps. Apple maps has become more accurate and helpful due to the following features.

Indoor Maps for Malls and Airports

You can now navigate through major shopping centers and airports. The maps provide a directory for stores and kiosks in malls, as well as in terminals. Maps show you where security is, food courts, restrooms, and specific stores. It will be much more efficient to find your way through a major mall and terminal.

Lane Guidance

It can be difficult to navigate while on the move. Lane Guidance assist drivers avoid missing exits by clearly showing which lanes they should travel in while navigating. Maps will assist with finding the way to your destination with guidance on which lane one should be in.

Speed Limit Indicator for Certain Roads

There is now the addition of speed limit notifications. You will be able to click on the speed limit to become aware of it in the area.

Do Not Disturb While Driving

Apple has included an expanding feature for their do not disturb feature by detecting when users are driving. The phone will lock itself to a do not disturb while driving mode and blocks any notifications.

One Handed Zoom

People can now zoom in and out of the Maps screen by double tapping on the Map screen with your thumb, but do not release your thumb from the screen on the second tap. Then zoom in, slide your thumb upwards, and slide it downwards to zoom out.

Light Guidance for Directions

Maps will now let you switch to a light guidance mode when reaching searching up directions or guidance. After tapping GO to start directions, a prompt at the top will pop up and state, Tap for light guidance.

Transit

Public transit information has improved for subways, buses, trains, and ferries. The Transit feature is customized for each city where it’s available. One can plan a route, pull up schedules, get step-by-step directions, and can ask Siri to guide you. One can find up-to-date information and reminders.

Proactive Suggestions

Maps predicts the places you are most likely to go and recommends the fastest route. Maps will actively find the fastest route for you no matter what time of day it is.

Apps within Maps

Maps now have third-party app extensions. Without leaving maps, one can now book a table at a restaurant and a ride to get you there. For emaple, if one makes a reservation through OpenTable, maps will automatically add it to your calendar. You can also book and pay for rides using Uber or Lyft. Apple Maps will show you the driver, the car, and the driver’s current location.
