--- title: zooming layout: component ---

A critical feature of GUI based operating systems is the ability is "zooming" by magnifying or shrinking content.

Typical Appearance

Zooming is typically characterized by content growing or shrinking in size.

Typical Behavior

Zooming typicall involves the magnification or minimizing of content in a window.

Key Characteristics

Feedback

With the exception of slow machines, the user receives immediate feedback when zooming through the visual change in size of the affected content.

Platform Specific Instances

When viewing content on a desktop, users can zoom in or out by way of the window menu or through predefined keyboard bindings. For example, Windows users may zoom in or out using the CTRL+ or CTRL- commands respectively.

Alternatively, users with a trackpad or viewing the content on a mobile smartphone are able to zoom out through the predefined gesture of pinching and zoom in by performing the gesture in reverse.
